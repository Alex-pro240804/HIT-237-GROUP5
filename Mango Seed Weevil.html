Mango Seed Weevil

Home Pest/Disease List About

Information

Mango seed weevil prevalence limits market alternatives. Weevils travel between orchards in infected seed and can fly through orchards; abandoned trees and fruit dumps are another source of weevil disease; fruit from mango seed weevil-free orchards can reach markets in Western Australia as well as China, Korea, the United States of America, or the United Arab Emirates.

Monitoring

Adults are difficult to locate; in the winter, look for adults under the bark; in new flushes, check for feeding damage; and in fruit, look for eggs. Harvest fruit and seeds and cut them to check for larvae.

Control

Removing all fruit from orchards, eliminating rejected fruit elsewhere, and burying fallen fruit and prunings before weevils appear are all effective ways to prevent mango seed weevils. Chemical controls should be used to target adult weevils on tree trunks and during the emergence, flushing, and fruiting stages. Larvae that emerge on fruit should be targeted with systemic insecticides.
